*Built top-down: Personas → Pillars → Clusters → Assets. The Q3 calendar
(`q3-2026-calendar.csv`) is derived from this; it is never a bottom-up pile of post ideas.*

**Grounded in:** `02-DOCS/wiki/brand/voice-guide.md` and `voice-dna.md` (the brand study —
Tend is a B2B fintech: cash-flow and treasury tooling for finance teams of small and
mid-size companies). Voice/tone is codified, so no STOP for `brand-voice`.

**Open gate — keywords:** no keyword/cluster research exists on disk. Clusters below are
derived from Tend's product surface and audience, NOT from search data. `target_keyword`

any flagship leaves the `brief` stage. This is the research half of the STOP gate, flagged
not skipped.

Team: 4 people (small). Cadence and WIP follow the small-team row of the content-engine
decision table: ~1 flagship/month, 4–6 supporting/week, ≥10 derivatives/flagship, WIP cap
3–4 in flight.

## Pillars (5) and clusters

Five pillars — inside the 4–6 band. Each maps to Tend's product surface and to the power-word
bank (`cash · runway · cleared · on time · same day · reconcile · trusted · holds`). Themes a
hype-driven fintech would not own; that is the point.

### 1. Cash visibility
*Seeing your true cash position in real time, against the bank, without spreadsheet lag.*
- `cash-position-basics` — what a real-time cash position is and why month-old numbers lie
- `forecasting-cash-flow` — building a rolling cash-flow forecast a finance lead trusts
- `bank-reconciliation` — reconciling against the bank without the monthly slog

### 2. Runway & treasury strategy
*Managing runway and idle cash so a small company stays steady, not breathless.*
- `runway-management` — measuring and extending runway with sourced numbers
- `idle-cash-yield` — what to do with idle cash safely (FDIC, sweep, the trade-offs named up front)
- `treasury-for-small-teams` — what "good treasury" looks like before you can afford a CFO

### 3. Paying & moving money
*Getting money out on time — payroll, vendors, transfers — with the cutoffs named.*
- `payroll-ops` — running payroll on time, every time, and what breaks it
- `transfer-cutoffs` — same-day vs next-morning, the 4pm ET line, and planning around it
- `vendor-payments` — paying vendors on schedule without manual chase

### 4. The close & reconciliation
*Closing the month and staying audit-ready without the controller burning a weekend.*
- `month-end-close` — a faster, calmer monthly close
- `multi-entity` — multi-entity treasury and consolidation without the mess
- `audit-readiness` — controls and records that hold up under audit

### 5. Trust, security & the cost of opacity
*Why legacy-bank opacity costs finance teams, and what plain, sourced, secure looks like.*
- `where-cash-is-held` — FDIC-insured partner-bank model, explained plainly
- `fees-and-cutoffs` — naming the fee/limit/cutoff before the benefit (a brand rule made content)
- `legacy-bank-opacity` — the real cost of jargon and hidden terms (our 80/20 irreverence, aimed at incumbents not the reader)

## Cadence & mix (Q3 = Jul–Sep 2026)

- **Flagship:** 1 / month → 3 flagships across Q3 (one per the highest-intent pillars).
- **Supporting:** ~5 / week (linkedin-post, x-thread, video-short, quote-graphic, carousel).
- **Proof:** 1 / month → 3 case studies / result pieces.
- **Slot mix:** ~70% evergreen / ~20% timely / ~10% experimental.
- **Open slots:** ~25% left as `reactive-open` (empty owner) for timely reactions.
- **Updating winners:** ~20% of weekly capacity reserved for refreshing existing pieces
  (encoded as `update-*` rows, mix=evergreen).
